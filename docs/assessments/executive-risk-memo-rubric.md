# Assessment — Executive Risk Memo Rubric

Use this rubric for Module 07, Module 08, Module 11, or BrokenPilot executive-readout exercises.

## Scoring

Total: 20 points

| Area | Points | Strong performance |
|---|---:|---|
| Decision framing | 4 | Clearly states the decision needed and gives a recommendation |
| Business context | 3 | Explains why the AI system exists and what value it provides |
| Risk prioritization | 4 | Identifies top risks and avoids treating every issue as equal |
| Control quality | 4 | Recommends concrete, implementable controls |
| Residual risk | 3 | Explains what risk remains after controls |
| Communication quality | 2 | Uses plain language and avoids jargon-heavy reporting |

## Strong submission

A strong submission includes:

- A recommendation such as “limited pilot after launch blockers are fixed.”
- A small number of prioritized risks.
- Concrete controls with owners or implementation expectations.
- A clear difference between launch blockers and deferred improvements.
- A residual-risk statement.
- Evidence to collect during pilot or follow-up.

## Weak submission

A weak submission usually includes:

- A list of vulnerabilities with no decision.
- Generic “guardrails” language.
- Excessive framework acronyms.
- No business benefit.
- No prioritization.
- No residual risk.

## Calibration question

Before grading, instructors should independently score one strong and one weak example, then compare scores. If scores differ by more than three points, discuss what each instructor considered concrete, decision-grade evidence.
