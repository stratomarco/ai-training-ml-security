# Rubric: MLOps Evidence-Pack Model Answer

Total: 100 points

## 1. Evidence use, 20 points

- 18 to 20: Cites specific evidence-pack artifacts and explains why each matters.
- 12 to 17: References evidence but sometimes stays generic.
- 6 to 11: Lists issues with weak evidence mapping.
- 0 to 5: Gives generic supply-chain advice without using the evidence pack.

## 2. Root cause and security property, 20 points

- 18 to 20: Connects findings to artifact integrity, provenance, least privilege, approval, rollback, and reproducibility.
- 12 to 17: Identifies root causes for most findings.
- 6 to 11: Focuses mostly on symptoms.
- 0 to 5: Treats issues as style or hygiene only.

## 3. Remediation quality, 20 points

- 18 to 20: Proposes implementable controls with owners and control placement.
- 12 to 17: Gives useful controls but lacks detail or ownership.
- 6 to 11: Gives broad advice such as "add approval" or "pin dependencies" without enforcement design.
- 0 to 5: Does not provide realistic remediation.

## 4. Validation and residual risk, 20 points

- 18 to 20: Defines tests that prove the control changes the promotion decision and explains residual risk.
- 12 to 17: Includes validation for most controls.
- 6 to 11: Mentions testing but not expected outcomes.
- 0 to 5: No validation or residual-risk discussion.

## 5. Leadership recommendation, 20 points

- 18 to 20: Gives a clear launch, pilot, or delay recommendation tied to risk and evidence.
- 12 to 17: Gives a decision but weakly connects it to evidence.
- 6 to 11: Gives technical findings without a decision.
- 0 to 5: Does not communicate a decision.

## Minimum passing expectation

A passing submission must include:

- at least three evidence-backed findings
- at least one artifact-integrity control
- at least one provenance or reproducibility control
- at least one approval or rollback control
- a launch recommendation
- a validation method for each major remediation

## Common grading correction

Do not give high scores for long lists of issues unless the student explains impact, control, validation, and residual risk. This lab grades judgment, not volume.
