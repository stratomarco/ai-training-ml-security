# 40-Hour Deliverables Map

This map keeps assessment realistic for a one-week course. It prevents the course from assigning every exercise as graded work.

## Required deliverables

| Day | Required deliverable | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | AI system context and trust-boundary diagram | Shows system understanding |
| Day 2 | LLM or RAG evidence log entry | Shows reproducible evidence |
| Day 3 | Tool permission matrix or executive risk memo | Shows control design or leadership communication |
| Day 4 | Rewritten finding | Shows report quality |
| Day 5 | BrokenPilot final report and presentation | Shows integrated assessment |

## Optional deliverables

Optional deliverables may be used for homework, longer cohorts, or advanced students:

- quizzes,
- additional DVAIA walkthroughs,
- additional privacy tabletop,
- adversarial ML test plan expansion,
- full supply-chain risk register,
- extended BrokenPilot implementation notes.

## Grading balance

| Component | Suggested weight |
|---|---:|
| Day 1 system context | 10 percent |
| Day 2 evidence log | 15 percent |
| Day 3 control or memo artifact | 15 percent |
| Day 4 rewritten finding | 20 percent |
| Day 5 BrokenPilot final report | 30 percent |
| Day 5 presentation and discussion | 10 percent |

## Assessment principle

Grade evidence, reasoning, control quality, validation, and communication. Do not grade students for finding vulnerabilities that the target cannot exhibit.
